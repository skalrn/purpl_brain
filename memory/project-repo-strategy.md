The purpl-brain repo is private. No external person has access to the code.

There is an unresolved strategic fork the user has not yet decided on:

1. **Open-source path** — Make the code public to signal engineering ability to recruiters and the developer community. Article becomes a portfolio/credibility piece.
2. **Product path** — Keep code closed, advertise the product to test whether it can generate real revenue traction. Article becomes top-of-funnel marketing with a waitlist or early access CTA.

**Why:** The two goals (recruiter signal vs. revenue) require different article tone, different CTAs, different demo strategies, and different decisions about what to expose.

**Decision made 2026-05-18:** Path B chosen — keep repo closed, treat this as a product launch. Goal is revenue traction, not recruiter signal.

**How to apply:** Article tone is outcome-focused (problem + proof it works), not implementation-focused. CTA is a waitlist or early access sign-up, never a repo link. Demo uses a synthetic brain on a public repo — not this repo's data. Do not suggest open-sourcing or exposing implementation details.
